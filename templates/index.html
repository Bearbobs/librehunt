Which language is this!?

Open Source? Libre?

Open Source, libre, and free are all terms that go hand in hand. They all loosely mean that the software is free, free as in free speech. You can see the code, help out, and assure yourself that it's secure. You can also make your own spin of it and give it to your friends and family! No legal EULA's to click Accept after multiple times, no restrictive licenses. Just free, and libre.

Operating Systems?

Operating systems are the systems running on your phone, laptop, and even Slow Cookers, such as macOS, or Windows. However, whereas only different versions of Windows and macOS exist, There are countless Linux distro's, (anyone can make their own linux system, changing the desktop, the theme, because there's more power to the user!)

Linux? Is that like macOS?

Well, sorta. Linux and macOS both are Unix based (technically) and so are related, but Apple uses proprietary (locked in) software for most of macOS, whereas Linux is free to use, free to edit, free to run, and free to share. You can even make your own linux spin, or your own linux distro!

Why should I use Linux?

There's a distro for everyone. From the nerd to the newbie.

There's a type for everyone. Advanced Linux distro's for the advanced users, and beginner distros for beginners. Distros also differ in their values, such as Ubuntu making Proprietary software easy to use for the average user, and Arch Linux, an advanced distro Keeping It Simple (KISS)! They also differ in their desktops, so a distro can have the simple GNOME desktop, or a customizable KDE Plasma desktop, or something lightweight and completely scriptable desktop like i3! Everything is customizable (you can run it straight from the pendrive without installing). Something for everyone.

100% Secure. Check for yourself.

Unlike in Mac or Windows, you can have your cake and eat it too! While remaining free (freedom wise as well as price) You can rest be assured your data does not get leaked to big corporations. The same, however, cannot be said for Windows. Plus, if you doubt me, don't take my word for it. You can see the source code, used to make the different components of the various distro's as well as the distro's themselves online, with a community for every project, welcoming contributions, ideas, and users! With Linux, you're secured for sure!

Do anything you want with it. Anything.

Since Linux is free (free as in free speech as well as free as in free beer) you can make copies and share it with your friends and family, make your own too, and run it, share it, copy it everywhere! From supercomputers to simple pendrives, Linux is everywhere.
